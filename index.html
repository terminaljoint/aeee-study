Calculus — Limits, Differentiation, Integration
9–10 Qs🔥 HIGHEST YIELD▼
Priority
5/5 — Most marks in Maths. Do this first.
Key limits
lim(x→0) sinx/x = 1Also: lim tanx/x = 1 and lim(x→0)(1−cosx)/x² = ½
lim(x→0)(1+x)^(1/x) = eDefinition of e. Variant: lim(1+1/n)ⁿ = e as n→∞
lim(x→a)(xⁿ−aⁿ)/(x−a) = naⁿ⁻¹Use when you see (xⁿ−aⁿ) form directly
Differentiation rules
d/dx(xⁿ) = nxⁿ⁻¹Power rule — used in almost every question
d/dx(uv) = u'v + uv'Product rule
d/dx(u/v) = (u'v − uv') / v²Quotient rule
Chain: dy/dx = dy/du · du/dxComposite function — most common differentiation type
d/dx(sinx)=cosx · d/dx(cosx)=−sinxAlso: d/dx(tanx)=sec²x · d/dx(secx)=secx·tanx
d/dx(eˣ)=eˣ · d/dx(ln x)=1/xExponential/log — tested every year
Integration formulas
∫xⁿ dx = xⁿ⁺¹/(n+1) + CPower rule for integration
∫sinx dx=−cosx+C · ∫cosx dx=sinx+CBasic trig integrals
∫eˣ dx=eˣ+C · ∫1/x dx=ln|x|+CStandard results — memorize both
∫1/(x²+a²)dx = (1/a)tan⁻¹(x/a)+CVery commonly tested — recognize this form fast
Area = ∫ₐᵇ |f(x)| dxArea between curve and x-axis — use |f(x)| if curve goes below x-axis
Practice MCQ — Exam Pattern
If f(x) = x³ − 3x, then f'(x) at x = 2 is:
A) 3
B) 9
C) 6
D) 12
f'(x) = 3x²−3. At x=2: 3(4)−3 = 12−3 = 9. Apply power rule term by term, then substitute.
Common Traps
  • Forgetting the "+C" in indefinite integration — AEEE often includes it as a wrong-answer trap option.
  • Mixing up d/dx(cosx) = −sinx (negative!) — very common error under exam pressure.
  • Area questions: if f(x) is negative in part of range, split the integral and take absolute values.
AEEE integration shortcut: if numerator = derivative of denominator → answer is ln|denominator| + C. Spot this pattern in 2 seconds.

Algebra — Matrices, Determinants, Quadratics, AP/GP
8–10 Qs🔥 HIGHEST YIELD▼
Priority
5/5 — Equal to Calculus in marks. Do this second.
Matrices & Determinants
|A| 2×2 = ad − bcFor matrix [[a,b],[c,d]] — expand along any row/column for 3×3
A⁻¹ = adj(A) / |A|Only if |A| ≠ 0. adj = transpose of cofactor matrix.
(AB)⁻¹ = B⁻¹A⁻¹Reversal law — order flips for inverse of product
Quadratic equations
x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2aQuadratic formula — memorize exactly as written
Sum of roots α+β = −b/aFor ax²+bx+c=0. Product αβ = c/a
D = b²−4acD>0: real distinct · D=0: real equal · D<0: complex conjugate
AP · GP · Series
AP: Tₙ = a+(n−1)d · S = n/2[2a+(n−1)d]a = first term, d = common difference. Sum of first n naturals = n(n+1)/2
GP: Tₙ = arⁿ⁻¹ · S = a(rⁿ−1)/(r−1)Infinite GP (|r|<1): S∞ = a/(1−r)
Σn = n(n+1)/2 · Σn² = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6Σn³ = [n(n+1)/2]² — the perfect square shortcut
Binomial: T(r+1) = C(n,r)·aⁿ⁻ʳ·bʳMiddle term: if n is even → T(n/2+1). Identify r from power of b.
Practice MCQ
Sum of roots of 2x² − 5x + 3 = 0 is:
A) 3/2
B) 5/2
C) −5/2
D) 5
Sum = −b/a = −(−5)/2 = 5/2. Product = c/a = 3/2. Don't forget the sign — −b/a, not b/a.
Common Traps
  • Sum of roots = −b/a NOT +b/a. The negative sign is the most frequently missed point.
  • For GP infinite sum: formula only valid when |r| < 1. Check this before applying.
  • For 3×3 determinant: expand correctly — alternate + and − signs along a row.
Memory Anchor

For quadratic ax²+bx+c: roots' Sum = −b/a, Product = c/a. Remember "SP = −b/a · c/a"

Coordinate Geometry — Lines, Circles, Conics
5–6 Qs▼
Priority
4/5 — Formulaic. Full marks if you remember the forms.
Straight lines
m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁)Slope. Line equations: y=mx+c (slope-intercept), y−y₁=m(x−x₁) (point-slope)
Dist point to line = |ax₁+by₁+c|/√(a²+b²)For line ax+by+c=0 and point (x₁,y₁)
Parallel: m₁=m₂ · Perp: m₁·m₂=−1Perpendicular slope = −1/m (negative reciprocal)
Angle between lines: tanθ = |m₁−m₂|/|1+m₁m₂|AEEE asks "acute angle between two lines" — use this formula
Circles
(x−h)²+(y−k)²=r²Standard form — centre (h,k), radius r
x²+y²+2gx+2fy+c=0General form — centre (−g,−f), radius = √(g²+f²−c)
Conics
Parabola y²=4ax: focus (a,0), directrix x=−aLength of latus rectum = 4a. Vertex at origin.
Ellipse x²/a²+y²/b²=1 (a>b)Vertices (±a,0); foci (±c,0) where c²=a²−b²; e=c/a<1
Hyperbola x²/a²−y²/b²=1Asymptotes y=±(b/a)x; foci (±c,0) where c²=a²+b²; e>1
Common Traps
  • General form of circle: centre is (−g,−f), NOT (+g,+f). The signs flip!
  • For ellipse: if b>a then vertices are on y-axis: (0,±b). Don't assume major axis is always x-axis.
Practice MCQ
The centre of circle x²+y²−6x+4y−12=0 is:
A) (−3, 2)
B) (3, −2)
C) (6, −4)
D) (3, 2)
2g=−6 → g=−3; 2f=4 → f=2. Centre = (−g,−f) = (3,−2). Radius = √(9+4+12) = 5.

Vectors, Probability & Binomial Distribution
5–7 Qs▼
Priority
4/5 — Vectors+Probability appear every paper.
Vectors
|a| = √(x²+y²+z²)Magnitude of a = xi+yj+zk
a·b = |a||b|cosθ = x₁x₂+y₁y₂+z₁z₂Dot product — use component form for calculation, magnitude form for angle
a×b: |a×b| = |a||b|sinθCross product magnitude. a⊥b ↔ a·b=0. a∥b ↔ a×b=0
Unit vector â = a / |a|Always divide vector by its magnitude to get unit vector
Probability
P(A) = n(A)/n(S)Basic definition. P(A') = 1−P(A) (complement)
P(A∪B) = P(A)+P(B)−P(A∩B)If mutually exclusive: P(A∩B)=0, so P(A∪B)=P(A)+P(B)
P(A|B) = P(A∩B)/P(B)Conditional probability. Bayes: P(A|B)=P(B|A)·P(A)/P(B)
Binomial P(X=r) = C(n,r)pʳqⁿ⁻ʳq=1−p. Mean=np. Variance=npq. SD=√(npq)
Common Traps
  • "At least 1" probability: always use 1 − P(none) instead of adding individual cases.
  • Independent events: P(A∩B)=P(A)·P(B). Mutually exclusive: P(A∩B)=0. These are different!

Permutations & Combinations
3–4 QsNEW▼
Priority
4/5 — Direct formula substitution. Quick marks.
nPr = n!/(n−r)!Arrangement — ORDER matters. "Arrange", "in a row", "first/second"
nCr = n!/[r!(n−r)!]Selection — ORDER doesn't matter. "Choose", "team", "committee"
nCr = nC(n−r) · nC0=nCn=1Symmetry: C(10,3) = C(10,7). Use to simplify large values.
n with repeats: n!/p!q!r!...p,q,r = counts of each repeated element. e.g. MISSISSIPPI
Circular: (n−1)!Necklace/bracelet (can flip): (n−1)!/2

At least one = Total − (none). e.g. "at least one girl" = All − All boys.

Distributing n identical into r distinct groups = C(n+r−1, r−1)

Practice MCQ
In how many ways can 5 people be seated around a circular table?
A) 120
B) 24
C) 60
D) 5
Circular arrangement = (n−1)! = (5−1)! = 4! = 24. For circular arrangements, one person is fixed as reference — so n! becomes (n−1)!.
Common Traps
  • Circular vs linear: circular = (n−1)!, NOT n!. Very common wrong-answer option.
  • "At least 2" = Total − (none) − (exactly 1). Don't just compute "none".

Priority
3/5 — 2-3 marks. Fast to learn.

Value of i⁴⁷ is:
A) 1
B) −1

Statistics & Mathematical Reasoning (Logic)
2–3 QsNEW▼
Priority
3/5 — Pure logic/recall. No calculation needed for reasoning.
Statistics
Mean = Σfx/Σfx = class midpoint for grouped data
Variance σ² = Σfx²/Σf − (x̄)²Compute this form — easier than deviation form
σ = √Variance · CV = (σ/x̄)×100CV = Coefficient of Variation (used for comparison between data sets)
Mathematical reasoning

Contrapositive of "P→Q" is "¬Q→¬P" — logically equivalent (same truth value)

Converse: "Q→P". Inverse: "¬P→¬Q". Both different from original.

p∧q (AND): T only when both T. p∨q (OR): F only when both F.

Negation: ¬(all X are Y) = "some X are not Y". ¬(some X are Y) = "no X are Y".

Practice MCQ
The contrapositive of "If it rains, the match is cancelled" is:
A) If it doesn't rain, the match is not cancelled
B) If the match is not cancelled, it didn't rain
C) If the match is cancelled, it rained
D) If it rains, the match is not cancelled
Contrapositive of P→Q is ¬Q→¬P. P = "it rains", Q = "match cancelled". ¬Q = "match not cancelled", ¬P = "it didn't rain" → "If match not cancelled, it didn't rain."

Modern Physics — Photoelectric, Atomic, Nuclear
4–5 Qs🔥 HIGHEST YIELD▼
Priority
5/5 — Half-life + Bohr model appear in EVERY paper.
Photoelectric effect
E = hf = hc/λPhoton energy. h=6.63×10⁻³⁴ J·s, c=3×10⁸ m/s
KE_max = hf − φEinstein's equation. φ = work function = hf₀ (threshold frequency)
eV_stop = KE_max · λ_dB = h/mvStopping potential. de Broglie wavelength of particle.
Bohr's atomic model
rₙ = 0.529n²/Z ÅOrbital radius. Doubles concept: if n doubles, r quadruples (n² dependence)
Eₙ = −13.6Z²/n² eVGround state (n=1) of H: −13.6 eV. Energy increases (less negative) as n increases.
ΔE = E_higher − E_lower = hfPhoton emitted when electron drops from higher to lower orbit
Nuclear physics
N = N₀(1/2)^(t/T½)Radioactive decay. After n half-lives: N = N₀/2ⁿ
λ = 0.693/T½ · N = N₀e^(−λt)λ = decay constant. Activity A = λN
Binding Energy = Δm × 931.5 MeVΔm = mass defect in amu. Higher BE/nucleon = more stable nucleus.
Practice MCQ
A radioactive substance has half-life 10 days. After 30 days, fraction remaining is:
A) 1/2
B) 1/4
C) 1/8
D) 1/16
Number of half-lives = 30/10 = 3. Remaining = (1/2)³ = 1/8. Every half-life halves the amount — do it 3 times.
Common Traps
  • Bohr energy levels: higher orbit = higher (less negative) energy, not lower. E₁
  • Photoelectric effect: intensity doesn't affect KE_max — only frequency does. AEEE loves testing this conceptual point.
  • Half-life: the answer is fraction remaining, not fraction decayed. 1/8 remaining = 7/8 decayed.

Electrostatics & Current Electricity
3–4 Qs▼
Priority
5/5 — Circuits appear every single year.
Electrostatics
F = kq₁q₂/r² · k=9×10⁹ N·m²/C²Coulomb's law. F ∝ 1/r² — halve distance = 4× force
E = kq/r² · V = kq/rE = field (vector), V = potential (scalar). E = −dV/dr
C = Q/V · Energy = ½CV² = Q²/2CSeries: 1/C=Σ(1/Cᵢ). Parallel: C=ΣCᵢ. Opposite to resistors!
Current electricity
V=IR · R_s=R₁+R₂ · 1/R_p=1/R₁+1/R₂Ohm's law. P=VI=I²R=V²/R (power formulas)
KCL: ΣI_in=ΣI_out (junction)KVL: ΣV=0 around any closed loop (sign: +ve if traversed with current)
Wheatstone bridge: P/Q=R/S (balanced)When balanced: no current through galvanometer. Cross-multiply to check.
Practice MCQ
Two capacitors 4μF and 6μF are connected in series. Equivalent capacitance is:
A) 10 μF
B) 2.4 μF
C) 5 μF
D) 1.2 μF
Series: 1/C = 1/4 + 1/6 = 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12. C = 12/5 = 2.4 μF. Remember: capacitors in series combine like resistors in parallel.
Common Traps
  • Capacitors in SERIES → smaller equivalent (like resistors in parallel). This is the opposite of resistors — extremely common trap.
  • KVL sign convention: when traversing in direction of current through resistor, voltage drops (−IR). Against current: +IR.
Memory Anchor

Capacitors vs Resistors: Capacitors — Series is smaller, Parallel is bigger. Resistors — exact opposite. C-S-P vs R-P-S.

Electromagnetic Induction & AC Circuits
3–4 Qs▼
Priority
4/5 — LCR resonance + RMS values appear every paper.
EMF = −dΦ/dt · Φ = BAcosθFaraday's law. Negative sign = Lenz's law (induced EMF opposes change).
Motional EMF = BlvConductor of length l moving at speed v ⊥ to field B
I_rms=I₀/√2 · V_rms=V₀/√2RMS = peak/√2. Average power P = V_rms·I_rms·cosφ
X_L=2πfL · X_C=1/2πfCReactance. Z=√(R²+(X_L−X_C)²). Resonance: f₀=1/2π√(LC)
Power factor = cosφ = R/ZAt resonance: Z=R, cosφ=1, power is maximum
Common Traps
  • RMS ≠ Average. V_rms = V₀/√2 ≈ 0.707V₀. Average = 2V₀/π ≈ 0.637V₀. Very different!
  • At resonance: Z is MINIMUM (=R), current is MAXIMUM. Not the other way around.
Practice MCQ
In an LCR circuit at resonance, impedance is equal to:
A) X_L
B) X_C
C) R
D) 0
At resonance X_L = X_C, so they cancel. Z = √(R² + 0²) = R. Impedance equals pure resistance at resonance.

Organic Chemistry — Named Reactions & Functional Groups
3–4 Qs🔥 HIGH YIELD▼
Priority
5/5 — Pure recall. 3-4 free marks with one revision session.
Must-know named reactions

Aldol condensation — two aldehydes/ketones → β-hydroxy carbonyl compound

Reagent: dilute NaOH. Reverse = retro-aldol.

Cannizzaro — aldehyde with no α-H → acid + alcohol (disproportionation)

Reagent: conc. NaOH. E.g. HCHO → CH₃OH + HCOONa

Grignard (RMgX) — reacts with carbonyl → adds R, gives alcohol after hydrolysis

Solvent: dry ether. Moisture destroys Grignard reagent.

SN1: tertiary carbon, polar protic solvent, racemization (carbocation intermediate)

SN2: primary carbon, polar aprotic solvent, inversion of configuration (backside attack)

Markovnikov: H adds to C with MORE H (electrophilic addition to alkene)

Hofmann bromamide: RCONH₂ + Br₂/NaOH → RNH₂ (amine with ONE FEWER carbon)

Functional group tests

Aldehyde: Tollen's (silver mirror) + Fehling's (brick red ppt). Ketone: fails both.

Carboxylic acid: effervescence with Na₂CO₃ (CO₂ gas released)

Alcohol (Lucas test): Tertiary = immediate turbidity. Secondary = ~5 min. Primary = no reaction.

Common Traps
  • Aldehyde vs Ketone: aldehyde gives Tollen's test. Ketone does NOT (no −H on carbonyl carbon).
  • Hofmann: product has ONE FEWER carbon than starting material. RCONH₂ → RNH₂, not R+1.
  • SN1 gives racemization. SN2 gives inversion (Walden inversion). They're opposite outcomes.

Solutions, Atomic Structure & Chemical Bonding
3–4 Qs▼
Priority
4/5 — Colligative properties + hybridization tested directly.
Colligative properties
ΔTb=Kb·m·i · ΔTf=Kf·m·ii=van't Hoff factor. i>1 for electrolytes (dissociation), i<1 for association.
π=iCRT · i=1+α(n−1)Osmotic pressure. α=degree of dissociation, n=ions from 1 formula unit
Atomic structure
Eₙ=−13.6Z²/n² eV · rₙ=0.529n²/Z ÅH-atom. For He⁺: Z=2. Ground state n=1.

Orbital filling order: 1s 2s 2p 3s 3p 4s 3d 4p 5s 4d... (Aufbau)

Mnemonic: "1s,2s,2p,3s,3p,4s,3d" — the 4s fills BEFORE 3d

Hund's rule: one e⁻ in each degenerate orbital before pairing (half-filled extra stability)

Pauli exclusion: no two electrons with same 4 quantum numbers

Chemical bonding & hybridization

sp: linear (180°) — BeCl₂, CO₂. sp²: trigonal planar (120°) — BF₃, SO₃. sp³: tetrahedral (109.5°) — CH₄, NH₃*

sp³d: trigonal bipyramidal (90°/120°) — PCl₅. sp³d²: octahedral (90°) — SF₆

VSEPR: lone pairs cause more repulsion → bond angles decrease. NH₃: 107° (1 lone pair). H₂O: 104.5° (2 lone pairs)

Common Traps
  • 4s fills before 3d (Aufbau), but 4s empties BEFORE 3d when forming transition metal ions. So Fe is [Ar]3d⁶4s², but Fe²⁺ is [Ar]3d⁶ (loses 4s first).
  • i factor: NaCl → 2 ions (i=2). CaCl₂ → 3 ions (i=3). MgSO₄ → 2 ions (i=2).

Periodic Table — Trends & p-Block Elements
3–4 QsNEW▼
Priority
5/5 — Direct "which is largest/most electronegative" = instant marks.
Periodic trends — direction is everything
Atomic radius: ↓ group · ← periodIncreases down (more shells) and left (fewer protons pull less)
Ionization energy: ↑ group · → periodEXCEPTIONS: Be>B (s-orbital more stable), N>O (half-filled 2p extra stable)
Electronegativity order: F>O>N>Cl>Br>C>HIncreases right and up. F is the most electronegative element.
Electron affinity: Cl>F (exception)F is smaller but small size causes lone pair repulsion → less EA than Cl
p-block key facts

Group 15 (N,P): NH₃ is pyramidal sp³ (107°, 1 lone pair). N≡N triple bond = very stable. PH₃ less basic than NH₃.

Group 16 (O,S): H₂O bent sp³ (104.5°, 2 lone pairs). H₂S lower bp than H₂O (H₂O has H-bonding).

Group 17 (Halogens): bond energy F-FBr-Br (F-F anomalously weak due to lone pair repulsion)

Group 18: first noble gas compound XeF₂. He = balloons (low density), Ar = light bulbs, Ne = neon signs

Practice MCQ
Which has higher ionization energy: N or O?
A) N
B) O
C) Both equal
D) Cannot be determined
N has higher IE than O (exception to the general trend). N has half-filled 2p³ configuration which is extra stable. O starts pairing electrons, causing repulsion and making it easier to remove one.
Memory Anchor — IE Exceptions

Normal trend: IE increases left to right. But: Be > B (s > p) and N > O (half-filled > paired). Remember: "Be Nice, Never Offer" = Be>B, N>O.

Articles

"a" before CONSONANT sounds: a book, a university ('yu' sound), a European

"an" before VOWEL sounds: an apple, an hour ('h' silent), an MBA

"the" for specific/known noun, unique things (the sun, the Eiffel Tower), superlatives (the best)

No article: general plural (Dogs are loyal), abstract nouns (Love is blind), languages, subjects

Prepositions (in/on/at)
in: months, years, cities, countriesin January, in 2026, in Chennai, in India
on: days, dates, specific surfaceson Monday, on 22nd April, on the table
at: specific time, place, addressat 9 AM, at the station, at 42 Main Street
Tense rules

Simple past: use "did" in question/negative; main verb stays base form (I did go, NOT I did went)

Present perfect: have/has + past participle. For actions with present relevance.

Subject-verb agreement: "The team is" (collective = singular). "The members are" (plural noun = plural verb)

  • "a university" not "an university" — because 'u' in university sounds like 'y' (consonant sound)
  • "an hour" not "a hour" — because 'h' in hour is silent (vowel sound)
